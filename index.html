Hi!

I’m Sish Ahemmed Shozol, a B.Sc. Statistics graduate from Khulna University with a CGPA of 3.75/4.00. My academic and research journey has focused on NLP, media framing, and explainable AI, leading to several international publications and conference papers. I’ve worked on fake news detection, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling using tools and techniques like BERT, LDA, and LIME. I’m skilled in Python, R, SQL, and frameworks like TensorFlow and Keras, with practical experience in data scraping (SNscrape, BeautifulSoup4), LLM prompting (Few-shot, CoT, ToT), and working with both traditional and transformer-based models. My projects have explored topics such as cancer framing, COVID-19 discourse, and the Russia-Ukraine war in media. I’m also active on Kaggle and the URI Online Judge, where I’ve solved over 100 problems, blending statistical expertise with technical proficiency to tackle real-world challenges.
